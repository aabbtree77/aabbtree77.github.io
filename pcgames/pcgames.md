# [Spintires: The Original Game (2014-2020)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spintires)

Off-road cargo delivery missions with a hypnotizing level of realism w.r.t. mud and vehicle physics. 

[The feud between Pavel Zagrebelnyy and Oovee](https://www.ign.com/articles/stuck-in-the-mud-how-a-tiny-beloved-driving-game-sparked-a-bizarre-decade-long-feud) lead to the mess of three subsequent games which are entirely missing the most interesting question to investigate: Will a particular setup (a truck + cargo + addons + skill) conquer the swamp or not? 

There should be at least several types of mud, the denser swamps which are better handled with tracked vehicles, and those which are best crossed by the vehicles with very large diameter (>1600mm), low pressure tires. 

I would want to see DT-30, Trom-8, and Trom-20, with a proper Sherp or Sherp Ark 10x10, not the one whose engine gets destroyed by a frontal collision with a tree trunk. The engine is not at the front of the vehicle!

More physics, geometry, details...

# [Inside (2016)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inside_(video_game))

This is a beautiful puzzle platformer which is especially interesting in how elaborately it renders [water, underwater, and the transition between the two.](https://blog.playdead.com/articles/inside_presentations/inside_publications.html) The game does run on Ubuntu with [wine and dxvk](https://github.com/doitsujin/dxvk/issues/3789).

Another noteworthy platformer is [Brothers: A Tale of Two Sons (2013, remade in 2024)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brothers:_A_Tale_of_Two_Sons) which introduces two cooperating characters/players instead of one.

# [Gokoban (2017)](https://github.com/danaugrs/gokoban)

One can create nontrivial puzzles by using only geometry. Like Rubik's cube or visibility/reconstruction sharades in engineering drawings. This game in particular is the famous [Sokoban (1982)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sokoban) extended to 3D. It demands pushing back cubes into the marked places by following surface and gravity laws.

[Stephen's Sausage Roll (2016)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen%27s_Sausage_Roll) is a nontrivial extension of Sokoban with rotational constraints.
