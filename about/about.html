I studied electrical engineering in Vilnius (Lithuania) from 1994 to 1999, followed by research in machine learning and industrial optimization. See my résumé or CV for more details.

Since 2014 I have been living in Vilnius off my savings. Aiming to become a software engineer. Take a look at some of my reports on github: Factory Repairs, Reliable IoT (Towards…), Volumetric Light in OpenGL.

Programming: Matlab, Scilab, LaTeX, HTML, CSS, Js. A bit of C, C++, Go, Python, OpenGL, Blender, git, MERN.

Math: Box method and its inverse, linear algebra, calculus.

Not good in: Speed tests, first impressions and marketing, pure anything (math, logic, sticking to one tool/concept for years…), vaguely defined research, big programs including personal career building. Quite miserable and non-productive when left to my own devices. A good project must have prehistory, compiler/tools, engineering, a concrete goal.

Decent with: Problems that demand some research/browsing and a week or so to solve. Can push myself at deadlines. Can work when paycheck is not adequate, but the project has some higher meaning, or the people are just friendly.

Can do wonders: When working in tandem on the same concrete thing with a friendly guru. This has been the case at least twice, once with Rodolphe Le Riche in Saint-Étienne, and also with Saulius Rakauskas in Vilnius. No one of us could pull this off alone.
