Александр Шпагин (2023)

There are some incredible people among us, with deeper vision, who aim to understand the world. Александр Шпагин (Alexander Shpagin) shares bits of his life as a moviegoer in the USSR and talks about cinema, its only two existing modes (indie vs. commercial), and how they have evolved from 1960s to 1980s. A painful process of creativity and search for meaning, and how A. Tarkovskij himself began to grasp the main idea behind his Mirror (1975) only after rendering the 16th version of it. Some real life details how metaphysics/religion seep into atheistic society the way people would reinvent dice or card games on an uninhabited island.

This interview, almost a monologue, packs a lot and is powerful as it is personal.
